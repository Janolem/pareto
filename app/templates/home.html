Welcome to the interactive exploration of Pareto fronts

The goal of this application is to deliver the complex information from optimization results with multiple objectives, and especially from optimizations that have a spatial context.

First, let us define potentially new terms:


Term Definition
Optimization Procedure in which we want to find the optimal solutions to one or several objectives
Objective function Function that quantifies the quality of a solution for one objective
Constraints Conditions that a solution to an optimization problem must satisfy
Pareto-front Frontier that contains all optimal solutions for a problem with conflicting objectives
Spatial configuration Solution represented in its spatial context

Next, we describe an example problem with two conflicting objectives.
Imagine you get a new job in a new city and you want to commute to work by bike.
You just recently got a new bike and don't want to break it, but the bike paths are scattered with pitholes.
You got a problem: you want to spend as little commuting time as possible, but you also want to avoid the pitholes.
Luckily you are able to formulize the problem, get the data and perform an optimization. How to decide?
